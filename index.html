About

Hello! And welcome to my page! My name is Lou Brian Lafuente, but everyone calls me Brian. I was born and raised in the state of Georgia. I studied Electrical Engineering Technology at Southern Polytechnic State University which is now merged with Kennesaw State University. I received my Bachelor's of Science Degree in 2014 from SPSU. During my college my career I took two programming language classes, which were Java/C++ and C/C#, that sparked my interest with programming and programming languages.


After parting ways from my previous job, I wanted to pursue something different and start to self-study the programming language Python through online sources. I heard about Atlanta Tech Village from a friend and then discovered that they housed a coding bootcamp called DigitalCrafts. I applied and the rest is history. Currently studying there and loving what I'm learning and curious to learn more that's out there that was probably not in the full-stack immersive program.

Projects

The Roaming Hobo

Remote Control Car controlled by via website and physical keys on keyboard. The technologies we used were Raspberry Pi, socket.io, johnny-five, and raspi-io to communicate to the car. The webpage was created with React.js and Firebase to store user's login. From the website, user's can control the RC Car by direction arrows on keyboard and also are able to see a live feed from the camera mounted onto Raspberry Pi.

The Budget Hobo

Creating a budegting app/webpage to help users keep track of their spending expenses. User's were able to register and log-in to be brought to a dashboard of their spending. Also user's were able to sign-in into their back account to obtain income or manually input it themselves. Their budget would be calculated as they inputted their expenses.
Technologies used were Express, MySQL, and Front-End elements.

The Proffesional Hobo

Front-end project of a travel webpage. It would give user's the option to look up flights and hotels by giving a departure and arrival city along with a date range to find a range of prices from different airlines.
Technologies that were used were HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and API to feedback data to the user. The name was inspired from a solo-traveler blog site.
